Note that the random subsample may mean that your results vary from this analysis.

Looking at the Probability row we see that the intercept, Bedrooms, Garage 2 Area, and Rooms are statistically insignificant. Ignoring these variables, the only variable with a negative coefficient is Age, which makes sense.

Taking a quick look at the models' fit:

  • Pseudo $R^2$ is 0.46 which is quite low compared to OLS. This means that only 46% of the variation in Sales Price can be explained by the model, which isn't good.
  • AIC is much lower than OLS, but this isn't a fair comparison because the dataset is also much smaller.
